D&D Cards63 × 88 mm — trading-card size

Click Print / PDF, then in the dialog: set Destination → Save as PDF, Margins → None, Scale → 100% (not "Fit"), turn Background graphics ON, and turn Headers and footers OFF. Cards print at exactly 63 × 88 mm. Cut along the grid lines — they slide into standard card sleeves and 9-pocket binder pages.

Cards always print organised by source, then level, then name — independent of how the library is grouped on screen. Tick the box on any cards to print just those; with none ticked, everything currently shown prints. Set Copies when printing on a card to print several of it (e.g. four Potions of Healing).

Double-sided: open Print options (the gear next to Print / PDF) and set Backs to By source or Uniform. Then print with two-sided / duplex on, flipping on the long edge. Front and back pages are interleaved and the backs are pre-mirrored so each lands behind its own card. For cards split with ---, tick “Split cards: description on both sides” to print the continuation on the back of the same card instead of a logo — so one physical card holds the whole thing.

Two decks: switch decks with the toggle at the top left. Each prints separately. For items, the description is the front and your artwork (or a watermark back showing the item type, name and rarity) is the reverse (set Backs → Image); items group and colour by rarity. Watermark strength/style is in Print options. Type icons by Lorc, Delapouite & contributors from game-icons.net (CC BY 3.0).

Import: loads a .json deck with options to merge (skip, overwrite, or keep duplicate names) or replace your library. Ready-to-import SRD spell and magic-item sets are available alongside the app.
